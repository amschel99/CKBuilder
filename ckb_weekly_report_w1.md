### Nervos Common Knowledge Base (CKB)

A layer 1 blockchain.

It is very hard to achieve decentralization, security, and scalability in a blockchain or a decentralized system.

One way to solve this is by having a multilayered system: L1s and L2s. This is what Ethereum did, and also what CKB does.

CKB itself is an L1 that focuses on security and decentralization. People can build L2s on top of it to make it more scalable.

It uses proof-of-work consensus.

CKByte is the native token. 1 CKByte entitles the holder to 1 byte of data on the blockchain. It is also used to pay for gas fees on the CKB blockchain.

CKBytes can also be staked in the Nervos DAO to earn staking rewards.

CKB-VM is a virtual machine based on RISC-V, and this is where the smart contracts written on CKB run.
